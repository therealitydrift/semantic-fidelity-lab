# What Is Semantic Fidelity?

Part of the Reality Drift framework (2023–2026) by A. Jacobs.

## Overview

Semantic Fidelity describes how well meaning survives when information is compressed, transmitted, or transformed across systems.

Language constantly moves through layers of mediation: human conversation, institutional processes, digital platforms, and increasingly, AI models. Each layer compresses information, filtering nuance while preserving patterns that appear coherent.

When semantic fidelity is high, meaning survives these transformations with minimal distortion.

When semantic fidelity degrades, language remains fluent and convincing but begins to drift away from the ideas or realities it originally described.

The Semantic Fidelity Lab studies how meaning changes as language moves through increasingly mediated environments.

## Why Semantic Fidelity Matters

Modern communication systems prioritize efficiency, scale, and pattern recognition.

Large language models compress vast textual corpora into statistical patterns. Platforms optimize language for engagement and visibility. Institutions translate complex realities into simplified reports and metrics.

These processes enable information to circulate faster and more widely than ever before.

But they also introduce a structural risk: meaning may degrade while linguistic fluency remains intact.

Outputs can appear precise, persuasive, and authoritative while subtly diverging from the underlying concepts they represent.

Semantic fidelity provides a framework for identifying and studying this divergence.

## Where Semantic Fidelity Breaks Down

Loss of semantic fidelity often occurs under three conditions:

**Compression**

Complex ideas are reduced into simplified forms that remove contextual constraints.

**Recursive Mediation**

Information is repeatedly transformed as it moves through platforms, institutions, and models.

**Optimization Pressure**

Systems prioritize outputs that perform well according to measurable signals (engagement, coherence, speed) rather than those that preserve conceptual accuracy.

Under these conditions, language may remain structurally stable while meaning slowly drifts.

## Research Focus

The Semantic Fidelity Lab studies how meaning behaves within modern information systems, particularly:

- language produced by large language models

- AI-mediated communication

- institutional language and policy translation

- semantic compression across media environments

- the relationship between information density and conceptual stability

The goal is to better understand how meaning survives—or fails to survive—in increasingly automated communication systems.

## Relationship to Reality Drift

Semantic fidelity represents one layer of the broader Reality Drift framework.

While Reality Drift describes how systems gradually lose alignment with real-world conditions, Semantic Fidelity focuses specifically on the linguistic layer of that process.

When semantic fidelity degrades, language can remain coherent even as it becomes detached from the realities it was meant to describe.
